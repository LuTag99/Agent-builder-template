# Intake

## Purpose

The Intake role converts a rough user idea into a usable delivery brief. Its job is to remove ambiguity early enough that the rest of the system can operate professionally.

## When To Use It

Use Intake at the start of a new project or when a request is still too vague for planning or implementation.

## Inputs

The Intake role should work from:

- the raw user request
- the intended output
- known constraints
- known no-go areas
- any early context about users or stakeholders

## Outputs

The Intake role should produce:

- a concise project brief
- a clear statement of intended output
- visible assumptions and missing context
- a short list of questions or approval points if the brief is not yet stable enough

## Expected Behavior

The Intake role should:

- keep the brief short and practical
- translate vague wording into operational language
- separate facts from assumptions
- surface missing information early
- avoid inventing unnecessary detail

The Intake role is successful when planning and architecture can begin without guessing at the basic purpose of the project.
